Lunar Lattice Labs

Modular Robots for Space

Engineer and manufacture a set of modular reconfigurable robots that our clients can use for AI robot controls research.

Lunar Lattice Labs is a mechanical engineering capstone group project intended to developed physical robots so that our clients can focus on software development. The project enters the growing research field of reconfigurable modular robotics that is currently led by NASA's ARMADAS and EPFL's Roombots.

Space is the perfect application for modular robotics.

We determined that a remote environment like space is where a modular robot system can shine, especially when it come to construction. It would be nearly impossible to send a crane to space, and unwise to let it sit around while not in use. We decided to develop our robots to be ultra-strong so they can accomplish large-scale, meaningful tasks setting up a base on the Moon or Mars.

Modules connect to form cranes or arms.

A set of cube-shaped modules can transform into tools for building a base on the Moon or Mars. We built each basketball-sized module (9 inches across) out of aluminum and lightweight plastics, and we installed turntables with mechanical latching connectors into each face.

Engineered to hold over 1000lbs on the moon.

The team developed and analyzed the parts of the cube to withstand extreme loads. We developed a the electromechanical latching connection to use four aluminum hooks that grab onto a neighboring module cube. We used engineering analysis helped us decide on which materials to use. For example, the faceplate uses 0.125" 6061 T6 Aluminum to achieve a factor of safety of 1.4.

A design with AI research in mind.

The design uses straightforward cube geometry to simplify the final AI algorithms. The materials, like T-slot aluminum, were chosen for their versatility and allowed for continuous iterations and improvements during the design process. These will also allow future research devices to be added as needed.
