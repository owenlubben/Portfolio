Polar Bench Inc.

Porch Bench with Cooled Cavity

Develop and patent a product to keep grocery deliveries cold while looking elegant.

Polar Bench is a startup based on the cooled porch bench product. The idea began in 2019 when my father, Vince, was not satisfied with bags of groceries splayed out on our porch in the sun before he came home from work. We did research into the market and found there was nothing to fill this gap. We began developing the product and filed for a patent. Version 1 went to market for a short time and the next version of Polar Bench is currently under development.

More people are ordering food than ever but the handoff hasn't improved.

We saw that grocery deliveries were on the rise, and one of the pain points of the process is when it gets to your door. What happens if its raining or you are still at work? What happens if it a hot summer day and your milk stays outside just a few minutes too long? We wanted to protect your packages and keep your house looking great, so we developed the Polar Bench.

Hidden under the seat is a temperature controlled cavity for holding packages.

In our prototypes we placed a minifridge on its back and set it underneath the seat opening of the bench. Future versions will have an app will temperture zone control within the box. This way if you recieve frozen items, refridgerated items, or room temp items; they can all be in the bench at once.

No one steals your packages and no one steals this idea.

The Polar Bench hides all of your packages from passersby and uses electronic locks to prevent porch pirates. We used intellectual property to protect the Polar Bench idea so no one else can make it. I was the main designer and drafter on the project and all of the patent diagrams are directly based on my original drawings.

Version 1 broke $100k in sales.

Over the course of one year, the most rudimentary version of the Polar Bench was produced. It was simply a quality-made patio table with an insulated bin and no electronics. It sold primarily at an outdoor furniture store in Minnesota, and did quite well. The future versions with full electronic capabilities are in development and we are looking to move production to a new manufacturing facility.

I was essential in designing the product and grew my entrepreneurship skills.

This project is an ongoing tale of entrepreneurship, design, intellectual property, branding, market research, and engineering. I learned to put engineering and design into perspective. Sometimes engineers can get caught up optimizing something or designers making something look or functional well, but they forgot to ask if the product is necessary and able to be made and sold. I find myself asking this question now and spending more time looking for projects that have a viable business backing.
