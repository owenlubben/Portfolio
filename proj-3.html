Solar Charging Station

To recommend changes for the Solar Charging Station by performing a user experience survey and test.

The Solar Charging Station (SCS) is a piece of a larger academic project at the the Sustainable Technologies Laboratory at Bochum University of Applied Sciences called SCiSusMob which stands for Smart City, Sustanable Mobility. I was matched with the lab through the DAAD RISE program that sends students to Germany for internships. My main task was to study the current solar charging station's user experience to make improvement for the upcoming version.

User tested in German and English with 36 participants.

I developed a user survey and test on Qualtrics based on previous academic work and conducted the study for 5 hours. We created flyers to advertise the event and provided free drinks to those who participated.

Users found Version 1 slow and confusing to use.

It was fairly easy to see the pain points of the station and where things could be improved. The version one of the station had two lids and an invisible card tap area. Even with the instructions that attempted to explain these difficult tasks, users could not figure out where to tap their nfc card to unlock the box and their arms would get caught between the multiple lids that were meant to protect the electronics from the rain.

4.6x faster when using inductive charging.

We implemented an inductive charging plate for e-scooters into the solar station and did and A/B test comparing it to wired charging. Users found the indutive charging to be very quick to use but sometime the scooter was tough to lift out of its slots.

78% of users preferred the new design.

Redesigned for intuitive and ergonomic use.

The next version of the Solar Charging Station (SCS 2.0) was based on a commercially available verson from Skyhook Solar. I desiged the control box that could be added to their design. It places every thing at a more comfortable height for the user, so they do not have to bend down. It recesses the lids and electronics to protect the electronics and charging cords from rain. The new, NFC card tap area is brightly colored and shaped liked a card, so it is obvious where the user should tap. The new lid is a single ightweight piece and holds itself up with gas struts so the users arms will never be smashed. The design is also mean to fit with the aestetics of the SunCrafter's design and uses bent metal with black powdercoating and matching geometry.

I was essential in interpreting the data to determine improvements.

I was able to figure out what needed to be studied and conduct the study in an international environment. I gained experience interpreting user test data to make product improvment recommendations.
